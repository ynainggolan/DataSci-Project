The Leading Causes:

By using the "New_York_City_Leading_Causes_Of_Death.csv" I am able to get all of the leading causes of death that has been recorded since 2007 to 2019. Using the same dataset it is also possible to categorize the causes into three categories: Self, Others, and Diseases. The "Self" is when the causes of death is the person itself, whether it be done by self harming or overdosing. The "Others" is when the cause of death is done by other people, whether it be homicide or accidents. Then the "Disease" is death caused by diseases whether it be cureable or non-curable.

In the image above we can see the frequency of cause of death being recorded throughout 2007 to 2019 (top). It is clear that the most frequent cause of death in NYC is diseases. Not only is it more frequent but it also have more kinds of diseases that is being recorded. Whereas the self and other being the cause of death, both of them are relatively equal throughout 2007 to 2019. But, what is interesting is that the "Self" as a cause of death has increases whereas "Others" has decreased, as can be seen on the bottom graph. Although the change in trend is comparatively small in comparison to the overall death, it is still concerning that more people is dying due to themselves since 2014 to 2020. We know that in 2020 drastic change has been made due to the global pandemic that is happening, and it seems that NYC populace becomes more burdened by the change which resulted in the increase of death done by themselves. Also a possible decrease in death due to other in 2020 is the fact that there is a lock down, which prevent other from interacting with each other physically as much.

Then on this graphs we can see the predictions of the three different causes of death. These three graphs shows the most contributed factor in the amount of death in NYC. As can bee see clearly it is Diseases, an the line on the graph suggested that it continue to be the most contributing cause. Meanwhile the other two graphs, "Self" and "Others", shows that they increasing ever so slightly on the graph; although the regression line predicts that they will contribute less as the cause of death. But it is clear that disesases are reulilng in the cause of death in NYC.

Car Crashes

Now that we have taken a look on the general cause of death in NYC let us analyze more on "Others" as the cause, more specifically motor vehicle. Since car accidents are rampant throught the country and it is a cause where people can collectively prevent.

This shows that although car crashes are rampant, the casualties it causes aren't that significant. However, although it is not significant the casualties of car crashes are fairly consistent. As can be seen on the graph to the left, there are a significant drop in car crashes between 2019 to 2021 and yet the casualties it produces are consistent. Which can be infered that the the amount of people getting killed is quite consistent no matter the amount of collision; while the amount of people injured is proportionate to the amount of collisions.

These graphs shows the trends of the total amount of people injured or killed based on the number of collisions per year. The graphs shows a positive regression line, meaning in the future the amount of people getting killed or injured based on the number of collisions will increases. Although the two previous graph made kind of shows us that number of peole killed is quite static while the amount of peole injured is proportionate to the amount of collisions, these graphs shows that both of them are in an increasing trend.
